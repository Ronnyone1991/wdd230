Caracas the branch of heaven

Caracas is the capital and the most populated city of Venezuela. Since the 19th century, it has been considered the center of political and economic power in Venezuela. It is located in the north-central coastal area of ​​the country, 12 km from the coast of the Caribbean Sea and is located within a mountainous valley. Although it is located near the coast, the city is located at almost 900 meters of altitude , separated from the central coast of Vargas, by the Cordillera de la Costa and which, as it passes through the urban nucleus, is constituted as the El Ávila National Park. The Caracas-La Guaira highway, serves as the main communication route between the city ​​and the coastal state of La Guaira, as well as the Simón Bolívar de Maiquetía International Airport and the port of La Guaira, the second port in the country.

The Metropolitan Area of ​​Caracas is the area of ​​Venezuela that has the highest nominal GDP and per capita in the country. The nominal GDP per capita amounts to USD 18,893 and a PPP GDP per capita of USD 32,710.23 The joint Human Development Index of the Metropolitan Area (Greater Caracas) in the three states where it is circumscribed are: (Capital District 0.806), ( Miranda State 0.793) and (La Guaira State 0.781). The Metropolitan District of Caracas, which includes five municipalities, has a population, according to the 2011 census, of 2,923,959 inhabitants.
